📏 Will it all fit? — 40L checked + 15L daypack

Yes, but the checked 40L is tight. Weight is comfortable (~12–14kg vs the 20kg limit) — volume is the constraint. To make it sit easily:

  • Buy body wash + shampoo locally — don't carry full bottles. Biggest space-saver.
  • Wear your bulkiest items flying — Chaco/runners + the R1 Air on the plane.
  • Compression cube for the R1 Air + spare layers.
  • Daypack soaks up overflow — Farpoint is 55L total if needed.

📐 Main-pack cross-section (back → front)

🪨 Against your back, lowest toiletry kit + liquids pouch (heavy, dense)
👕 Core volume clothing cubes + compression cube
👟 Base corners shoe bag + dry bag
🧺 Top / front towel · poncho · sarong · flat laundry bag — grab-first

✅ Pack-day order of operations

  1. ~48h before: permethrin-treat the Outlier pants, socks & cap outdoors; let them dry fully.
  2. Toiletries: full-size bottles into the Bellroy kit — no decanting. Sunscreens + repellent into the ProCase liquids pouch, double-bagged.
  3. Cube the clothes: tees + underwear + socks + shorts in the Reveal cubes; R1 Air in the compression cube.
  4. Build the DAYPACK first: med cube, tech pouch, passport/docs pouch, 1 spare outfit, day-access pocket. ✅ power bank + all meds here, NOT checked.
  5. Build the MAIN pack: toiletry kit + liquids low against the back → clothing cubes core → shoe bag + dry bag base corners → soft items pad gaps → towel/poncho/sarong on top.
  6. AirTags: one in the main pack, one in the daypack.
  7. Departure morning: doctor's letters + anxiety/flight med (original labelled box) into the med pouch.
  8. Weigh both bags (Jetstar 7kg / Scoot 10kg carry-on + 20kg checked).
  9. Final scan: passport · wallet · phone · meds · chargers · glasses · keys.
